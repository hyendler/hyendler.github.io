Hashes & Arrays

11/19/15

I was excited to finally start up Ruby again after HTML & CSS, as it allows more room for playing with logic. Hashes and arrays are especially dear - they are essentially the same as matrices from math class (can't wait until we are dealing with big sets of data and nested arrays!). Manipulating data to produce useful results

But why are hashes and arrays that interesting and important? Arrays are great for storing lists of singular elements, whereas hashes can store key-value pairs, which allow for a more flexible relationship because you can give your element a specific name or 'key'. Once can almost think of an array like a hash, but has an automatic relationship determined by it's index number.

Even though hashes and arrays are different from each other, manipulating, accessing and iterating over them are nearly the same.
